About Deneb

Deneb is Sam Rowe's personal tech blog. It explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and software engineering — with honesty, structure, and a healthy amount of scepticism.

The Name

Deneb is one of the most luminous stars in the Milky Way — distant, understated, and often overlooked in favour of flashier constellations. That felt right for a blog that aims to shine a steady light on technology, rather than chase the glare of hype cycles.

The Author

Sam Rowe is a software engineer with a focus on the full software development lifecycle. He is interested in how AI is reshaping the way software is designed, built, tested, and deployed — and what that means for the humans doing that work.

You can find him on GitHub and LinkedIn.

The Mission

This blog exists to document the honest experience of building software in the age of AI. Not the marketing version. Not the conference keynote version. The actual version — with the dead ends, the unexpected wins, and the questions that don't have clean answers yet.

Topics include:

  • AI in the software development lifecycle
  • The changing role of the software engineer
  • Tools, workflows, and patterns that actually work
  • The craft of building things well

AI Transparency

Posts on this blog are written with AI assistance. That is part of the story, not something to obscure. Every post that involves AI collaboration is clearly labelled, and the nature of that collaboration is explained where relevant. The thinking is human. The refinement is a partnership.

Insight

AI-assisted writing is not a shortcut — it is a different kind of work. The hardest part is still forming the opinion worth writing about.

Confidence: High
