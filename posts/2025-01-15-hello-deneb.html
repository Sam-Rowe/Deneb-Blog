Hello, Deneb: What This Blog Is Here to Do

15 January 2025 · 5 min read · AI-assisted

The internet is full of AI takes. Most are either breathlessly optimistic or needlessly cynical. This is neither.

Deneb is a blog about building software in the age of AI — written by a software engineer who is actually doing it, not just commenting on it. The goal is honest, structured thinking. Not hype, not doom, not vapid use cases. Actual insight from the work itself.

Why "Deneb"?

Deneb is one of the most luminous stars in the Milky Way. It sits at the tail of Cygnus — often overlooked in favour of the more prominent stars nearby, yet significantly more powerful than it appears. That felt like the right metaphor for this project.

A lot of what matters in software engineering does not announce itself loudly. The practices that actually improve quality, the tools that actually change how you work, the patterns that actually hold up at scale — they tend to be the ones that require patience and attention to see clearly. That is what this blog is here to illuminate.

What This Blog Covers

The focus is the software development lifecycle — design, development, testing, deployment, and everything in between. Specifically, how AI is reshaping each of those stages, and what it means for the engineers doing that work.

Posts will cover:

  • AI in the SDLC — where it is genuinely useful, where it is oversold, and what the gap looks like in practice
  • Engineering craft — the fundamentals that remain important even as the tooling changes around them
  • Tools and workflows — honest assessments of what actually changes how you work, not just what looks good in a demo
  • The human side — what it feels like to be a software engineer right now, and where the role is heading

Insight

The most significant shifts in software engineering rarely announce themselves as revolutions. They arrive as slightly better defaults — until one day the old way feels obviously wrong.

Confidence: High

On AI Assistance

This blog is written with AI assistance. That is not a caveat — it is part of the point. Using AI to write about AI in software development is not ironic; it is the honest framing.

Every post will be transparent about where and how AI was involved. The thinking, the opinions, and the editorial judgement are human. The collaboration is where the writing gets refined — structured, tightened, challenged. That process is itself worth writing about, and it will be.

Posts that involve AI assistance are marked clearly. Some will include notes on how the collaboration worked, what the AI contributed, and where it pushed back or surprised.

What to Expect

Posts will be structured, opinionated, and grounded. The aim is not volume — it is quality and honesty. Each post will have a clear argument, evidence or reasoning behind it, and a point of view worth disagreeing with.

If something changes — if I am wrong, if a tool I praised turns out to be a dead end, if the landscape shifts — that will be written about too. The version history is part of the story.

Welcome to Deneb.
